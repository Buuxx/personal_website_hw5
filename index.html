About Me

Bioinformatics and Bisiness Economics Dual Major at UC San Diego

Growing up in Beijing, China, I'm currently pursuing my dual degree in Bioinformatics and Business Economics at UC San Diego. Graduating in Winter 2025, I will soon become a Research Assistant working for labs that focus on population genomics.


Seems opposite to what my career interests lead me to, I'm emthusiast in photography and film making.


My Research Interest


"Bioinformatics, as related to genetics and genomics, is a scientific subdiscipline that involves using computer technology to collect, store, analyze and disseminate biological data and information, such as DNA and amino acid sequences or annotations about those sequences. Scientists and clinicians use databases that organize and index such biological information to increase our understanding of health and disease and, in certain cases, as part of medical care." --NIH


I am interested in bioinformatics, particularly in fields such as population genomics and immunotherapy. I am eager to engage with more research labs to further explore and refine my specific areas of interest. Currently, I am contributing to the development of a pangenome browser as part of the Gymrek Lab at UC San Diego.
